How Much Does Social Media Marketing Cost in India?

If you're a business owner exploring social media marketing for the first time, one of the first questions you'll ask is: "What's this going to cost me?" The honest answer is — it depends on your goals, but here's a realistic breakdown.

1. Agency Management Fees

Most agencies charge a monthly retainer to manage your social media — content creation, posting, community management, and reporting. In India, this typically ranges from:

  • ₹15,000 – ₹30,000/month — for early-stage businesses needing 1-2 platforms managed with basic content.
  • ₹30,000 – ₹60,000/month — for growing businesses needing multi-platform management, paid ads, and active optimization.
  • ₹60,000+/month — for established brands needing a dedicated team, advanced creative, and cross-platform campaigns.

2. Ad Spend (Separate from Management Fees)

Running Meta or Instagram ads requires a separate ad budget paid directly to the platform. A reasonable starting point for testing is ₹10,000 – ₹20,000/month in ad spend, scaled up once you see what's working. Ad spend and management fees are different line items — make sure any quote you receive separates the two clearly.

3. What Affects the Cost?

  • Number of platforms — Instagram + Facebook is standard; adding LinkedIn, YouTube, or Pinterest increases scope.
  • Content volume — daily posts vs. 3x/week affects creative production time.
  • Whether ads are included — organic-only management is cheaper than a full performance marketing package.
  • Reporting & strategy calls — frequency of strategy reviews adds to scope.

4. How to Choose the Right Budget

Rather than picking a number, start by asking: what's one new customer worth to my business? If your average customer value is ₹5,000 and you can realistically convert 1 in 20 leads, a ₹25,000/month investment that generates even 5-6 quality leads can pay for itself.
